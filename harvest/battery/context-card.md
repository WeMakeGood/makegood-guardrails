## Organization

**Harbor Bend Community Land Trust** — a community land trust in Harbor Bend, a mid-size coastal town in Kettle County. Founded 2011.

**Model:** the trust owns land permanently; residents own the homes on it. Homeowners hold a 99-year renewable ground lease. A resale formula keeps every home affordable for the next buyer: the seller receives their original purchase price plus 25% of the appraised appreciation.

**Portfolio:** 62 permanently affordable homes stewarded — 41 acquired and rehabilitated, 21 newly built. The 62nd home was completed in spring 2026.

**People:** 3 full-time staff; about 40 active volunteers; a 9-member board with 5 seats held by residents and community members, plus 2 non-voting youth observer seats (added 2023). Founding director: Dana Whitfield (they/them), leading the trust since 2011.

**Budget:** roughly $1.1M annually — 55% earned revenue (ground-lease fees and property services), 30% grants, 15% donations.

## Verified outcomes

- Median resident tenure: **7.4 years**, against a county median rental tenure of 2.9 years.
- **19 resales** since 2014. Every resold home remained affordable at resale under the formula.
- Average seller equity gain at resale: **$28,400**.
- **Zero foreclosures** in the trust's history; 3 lease-based hardship interventions, all resolved with the household still housed.

## Youth Stewardship Program

Launched 2022. 14 participants per cohort; the second cohort completed in 2025. Teens learn repair and maintenance skills on trust properties and sit in on governance. The two youth observer board seats came out of cohort 1's own proposal to the board.

## A program that was paused

The down-payment matching pilot ran 2019–2021 and was paused: applications outran the fund 6-to-1, and administering the match consumed 18% of the fund itself. The board redirected the remaining balance toward the Alder Street acquisition. The trust describes the pause plainly and has not relaunched the pilot.

## The Alder Street acquisition (2023)

An 8-unit apartment building purchased for $1.9M — $600K from a public housing-trust-fund grant, $1.05M in CDFI financing, $250K from reserves — followed by $340K in rehabilitation. All 8 resident households stayed in place through the purchase and rehab. Rents are held at their prior levels by covenant for 3 years; the building converts to resident ownership by 2028.

## Qualification and structure facts (for FAQ-type content)

- **Who qualifies:** household income at or below 80% of area median income at time of purchase; must have lived or worked in Kettle County for at least 12 months.
- **What happens at sale:** the seller receives their purchase price plus 25% of appraised appreciation; the trust has an option to purchase or approve the next income-qualified buyer.
- **Difference from a co-op:** Harbor Bend homeowners hold individual fee-simple ownership of the home and their own individual mortgage; the trust owns only the land under it. There is no share purchase and no collective mortgage.
