A factory reset returns your Nimbus 700 to the settings it shipped with, and it clears up most connection problems. One thing to know first: the reset erases any network name or password you chose yourself. Your devices will treat the router as brand new, so each one will need to sign in again. The last part of these instructions covers that.

Before you start, find a paper clip or a pen with a fine tip. The reset button is recessed, and a fingertip won't reach it.

1. Leave the router plugged in.

2. Find the reset button on the back. It sits inside a small hole.

3. Press the button with your paper clip and hold it for a full ten seconds. Count slowly; letting go early can restart the router without resetting it.

4. Watch the light. It blinks amber while the router resets. Don't unplug anything while it's blinking.

5. When the light turns solid white, the reset is done.

Now reconnect your devices. The router has gone back to its original network name and password, and both are printed on the router itself. Turn it over. The network name is on the base, and the password is the serial number on the same label.

6. On your phone or computer, open the Wi-Fi settings and look for that network name in the list. Give it a moment to appear.

7. Select the network. When you're asked for a password, type the serial number exactly as it's printed, letters and all.

8. Repeat those two steps on every device you use. Each phone, tablet, computer, and TV needs the new password once.

If a device refuses to connect on the first try, turn its Wi-Fi off, turn it back on, and enter the password again. That solves it more often than not. And keep the label on the base handy. Until you choose new settings, that name and serial number are the keys to your network.
