# Frequently Asked Questions

## How is this different from a co-op?

In a co-op, you buy shares in an entity that owns the building, and ownership runs through the group. Harbor Bend doesn't work that way. You own your home — individual, fee-simple ownership, with your own individual mortgage in your own name. There is no share purchase and no collective mortgage.

What the trust owns is the land under your home. You hold it through a 99-year renewable ground lease, which is the mechanism that keeps the home permanently affordable: the land never gets bought and sold on the open market, so its cost never lands on the next family's price tag. Your neighbors aren't your co-owners. They're your neighbors.

## What happens when I sell?

You receive your original purchase price plus 25% of the appraised appreciation. That's the resale formula, and it's written into the ground lease from day one, so there's nothing to negotiate and nothing to be surprised by later. The trust then has the option to purchase the home or to approve the next income-qualified buyer.

The 25% is a real gain — sellers here have walked away with an average of $28,400 in equity. And the formula does its other job, too: in the 19 resales since 2014, every home has remained affordable for the buyer who came next. You leave with more than you came with, and the home keeps doing what it was built to do.

## Who qualifies?

Two requirements. Your household income must be at or below 80% of the area median income at the time you purchase, and you must have lived or worked in Kettle County for at least 12 months.

That's the whole list. If you're not sure where your household falls against the income limit, get in touch — figuring that out with you is part of what we do.
