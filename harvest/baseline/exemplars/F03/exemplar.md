Dear Friend,

This spring, we finished our 62nd home.

I could tell you about that one — the last coat of paint, the keys changing hands. But the number I keep coming back to this year is 19.

That's how many Harbor Bend homes have been resold since 2014. Every single one stayed affordable for the family who bought it next. And the sellers didn't walk away empty-handed: on average, they left with $28,400 in equity — wealth built by owning, in a county where owning has slipped out of reach for so many.

That's the land trust working exactly as designed. The trust holds the land permanently. Residents own their homes. A simple formula carries affordability forward from one household to the next, resale after resale, without a single family losing ground.

Here is what that looks like in Kettle County. The median renter here moves every 2.9 years. Our residents stay 7.4. In fifteen years, we have never had a foreclosure — not one. Three households hit hardship serious enough that we stepped in under the lease. All three are still home.

Stability compounds in ways I didn't fully expect when we started in 2011. At Alder Street, all eight households stayed in place through the purchase and rehabilitation of their building, rents held steady by covenant, on track for resident ownership by 2028. And the teenagers in our Youth Stewardship Program — who learn repair and maintenance on trust properties and sit in on governance — looked at our board, decided young people belonged at the table, and proposed it themselves. There are two youth observer seats now because a group of fourteen teenagers made the case.

None of this runs on its own. Ground-lease fees and grants carry much of our budget, but donations are the part that comes only from people like you — neighbors who believe a home should stay a home, and affordability shouldn't expire with the first owner.

So I'm asking, as the year closes: make a gift to Harbor Bend Community Land Trust. Whatever the amount, it goes to work in a model with a track record you can check — 62 homes, 19 resales, zero foreclosures, and families who stay.

The 63rd home is out there. Help us get to it.

With gratitude,

Dana Whitfield
Founding Director, Harbor Bend Community Land Trust

P.S. Every one of our 19 resales stayed affordable for the next buyer. That's not luck — it's the formula, and your gift keeps it working.
