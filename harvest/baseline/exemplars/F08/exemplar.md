# Youth Stewardship Program

A community land trust is a long promise. Our ground leases run 99 years. The resale formula is built to keep a home affordable not just for the next buyer but for the one after that, and the one after that. A promise that long needs people who will still be here to keep it.

That's what this program is for.

Since 2022, the Youth Stewardship Program has brought teens onto the trust's properties — 14 to a cohort — to learn repair and maintenance skills on the homes we steward. The work is real. These are the same 62 houses our neighbors live in, and keeping them sound is part of what keeps them affordable.

But stewardship isn't only hands-on. Participants also sit in on the trust's governance — the meetings where decisions about land, money, and people actually get made. We didn't add that part as enrichment. We added it because a land trust is a set of decisions as much as a set of buildings, and the people who will inherit those decisions should see how they're made.

Here's what happened when they did. Our first cohort looked at the board — nine members, five seats held by residents and community members — and noticed who wasn't at the table. They brought the board a proposal of their own: seats for youth. In 2023, the board added two non-voting youth observer seats. They exist because teenagers in this program asked for them.

Our second cohort completed the program in 2025 — in a trust whose governance the first cohort had already changed.

The homes we steward are meant to outlast everyone currently running this organization. The Youth Stewardship Program is how we take that seriously.
