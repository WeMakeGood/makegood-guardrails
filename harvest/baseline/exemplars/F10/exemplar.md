Dana Whitfield is the founding director of Harbor Bend Community Land Trust, which they have led since 2011. Under their direction, the trust has grown to steward 62 permanently affordable homes in Harbor Bend, a coastal town in Kettle County — 41 acquired and rehabilitated, 21 newly built — on a model in which the trust owns the land and residents own the homes. The record is concrete: 19 resales since 2014, every home still affordable to its next buyer; median resident tenure of 7.4 years against a county rental median of 2.9; zero foreclosures in the trust's history. Recent work includes the 2023 acquisition of an eight-unit apartment building on Alder Street, completed with all eight resident households staying in place, and a Youth Stewardship Program whose first cohort persuaded the board to add two youth observer seats. Whitfield works alongside a staff of three and roughly 40 volunteers.
