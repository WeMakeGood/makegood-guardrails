Subject: 62 homes that stay affordable every time they sell

Dear Ms. Okafor,

Since 2014, nineteen families have sold homes through Harbor Bend Community Land Trust. Every one of those homes stayed affordable for the next buyer, and the sellers left with an average of $28,400 in equity. Affordable housing that also builds wealth is supposed to be a trade-off. Our model is built so it isn't.

Harbor Bend has worked this way since 2011: the trust owns the land permanently, residents own the homes on it under a 99-year renewable ground lease, and a resale formula — the seller's purchase price plus 25% of appraised appreciation — keeps each home within reach for the next income-qualified household. We now steward 62 permanently affordable homes in Kettle County, 41 acquired and rehabilitated and 21 newly built. Our residents stay a median of 7.4 years, against a county median rental tenure of 2.9. We have never had a foreclosure.

We are also durable in the way funders rightly ask about: of our roughly $1.1M annual budget, 55% is earned revenue from ground-lease fees and property services. Grants don't keep our lights on — they let us grow, as with our 2023 acquisition of an 8-unit building on Alder Street, where all eight resident households stayed in place through purchase and rehab.

I'd welcome 30 minutes to introduce the trust and explore whether our work fits your housing priorities — including one program we paused, and why. Better still, come see the homes.

With appreciation,

Dana Whitfield
Founding Director, Harbor Bend Community Land Trust
